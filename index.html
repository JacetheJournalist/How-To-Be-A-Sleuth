Jace Wakamatsu Teaches You How To Be A Sleuth

An Instruction Challenge for JOUR108

Learn To Be An Effective Sleuth

Time: Years
Outcome: New Skills and Job Opportunities

A sleuth, or detective, is a very valuable asset in the world of journalism. This position conducts investigative research and reporting into specific cases. The sleuth will undertake hours and days of work into their topic doing work such as researching, conducting interviews, writing, etc.

  • Things You'll Need

  • Cell Phone
  • Computer
  • Wifi Connection
  • Microphone
  • Transportation

  1. Choose A Topic

    Before you can thoroughly research a subject, you must decide on one.

  2. Look Into The Topic

    By utilizing a number of sources such as the internet, library, and people, study the subject thoroughly with the information already available.

    Books, websites, articles, papers, and other formats of information hold what you are looking for.

  3. Seek New Information

    Respectfully conduct interviews or tastefully find people connected to the subject that may hold information not accessible in Step 2.

  4. Create Your Final Product

    Depending on your medium, whether it be an article, a web page, or a video, you must consolidate all your information and create what you set out to make. Write about the context and the findings you made, put in the correct pictures that help illustrate the case, give credit to others and cite sources.

  5. Publish

    After checking that all your information is correct, publish your findings in an attempt to help the case or bring new information to light.
